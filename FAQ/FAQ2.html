Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to own a car to start spotting?
Absolutely not! You pretty much only need to have your camera or phone ready, just mark down the locations and head on there. Of course it will be easier to get to more secluded locations with a car.

What kind of lens do I need for spotting? Will owning just a phone suffice?
Most of those spotting locations are right next to the road and you can just use an ordinary smartphone (with 10mm - 40mm zoom) to take pictures. Of course a dedicated specialty camera and special lens will produce better results.

Where can I find a group to go spotting together?
The best way to connect with local car enthusiasts and photographers is through social media. Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and focused car forums are the best bet.

How safe is car spotting?
This answer depends on the location and event you attend - time is important too. Trying to spot cars during the day is usually a safer, better idea since most of aggressive driving and street racing occurs during the night. Also, Stay behind any guard-rails or medians if there are any, do not venture out into oncoming traffic.
Is it reccomended to stop and ride with strangers, even if they are car enthusiasts?
Unfortunately no. We do not suggest that you do this. Also, please refrain from sending strangers your personal information when connecting, keep it within the realm of social media only.

How can I contact you if I want to know more?
Please reach out to us @bayareacarspotters on social media.
